Special Pokémon GameBoy Color editions

These unique consoles were typically released in celebration of the release of a new Pokémon game or movie, or to commemorate a special occasion. Today, these special edition consoles are highly sought after by collectors and fans alike, with some models commanding high prices on the secondary market due to their rarity and unique designs. Maintaining the GameBoy Cartridge and Console

Cleaning

Cleaning the cartridge is an essential step in ensuring that it remains functional for a long time. Dirt, dust, and other debris can accumulate over time and interfere with the cartridge's ability to connect to the console, causing it to glitch or not work at all. To clean a GameBoy Color cartridge, you will need a soft, dry cloth and rubbing alcohol. First, remove the cartridge from the console and use the dry cloth to wipe away any visible debris. Then, dip the cloth in rubbing alcohol and gently wipe the contacts on the bottom of the cartridge, being careful not to damage them. Repeat this process until the contacts are clean. Once the cleaning is complete, let the cartridge air dry for a few minutes before inserting it back into the console. It is important to note that cleaning the cartridge should be done sparingly and only when necessary, as excessive cleaning can damage the contacts and render the cartridge unusable.

Replacing the battery

The original Pokémon games for the Game Boy have a small battery inside the cartridge that powers the game's internal clock and allows the player to save their progress. Since these cartridges were produced more than 20 years ago, you might need to replace it in order to ensure that your Pokémon game will continue to function properly and you won't lose any saved progress. Here is a short guide on how you can do that:

  1. First, you'll need to open up the cartridge. To do this, you can use a special tool called a tri-wing screwdriver or a small flathead screwdriver. Be sure to unscrew the screws carefully and keep track of them so you don't lose them.
  2. Once the cartridge is open, you'll see the battery. It's a small circular battery that's soldered onto the circuit board. You'll need a soldering iron to remove the old battery and solder a new one onto the board.
  3. When you have the new battery, place it in the same spot as the old one and use your soldering iron to connect it to the circuit board. Be careful not to hold the iron in one spot for too long as this can damage the cartridge.
  4. Once the new battery is connected, close up the cartridge and screw it back together.

Fixing an old GameBoy

Fixing specific problems with your Game Boy can be a complex process and requires specific knowledge and tools. Unfortunately, we cannot cover every single issue here, but we want to share an inspiring video that showcases the restoration of a Game Boy that was in very bad shape. This video may give you some ideas and inspiration for your own restoration projects, but please keep in mind that repairing your own Game Boy can be risky, and we always recommend seeking the help of a professional if you are not confident in your ability to fix it yourself.

Summary of the video

In the video, you can see an old, pretty beat-up Pokémon special edition GameBoy Color. The repairman is holding it up to the camera, with a white table in the background. There are several close-ups to show how dirty it is. When the repairman tries to turn it on, it does not work.

First, he unscrews and removes the back panel of the GameBoy, then removes the motherboard, which is also in pretty bad shape. He then separates the buttons from the panel and removes the lens of the front panel. Next, he connects the motherboard to a power source to test it. He rubs it with isopropyl alcohol and then inserts a Pokémon Yellow cartridge. Now, he is able to turn on the system, but it still cannot read the game cartridge. He proceeds to clean the cartridge slot with a toothbrush.

Now the game starts up correctly, but one of the buttons is not working. He turns the system off and uses a voltage tester to determine where the break in the connection is. After he finds it, he solders a piece of wire to close the gap in the connection, which finally fixes the button.

He then removes a bit of sticker residue from the front panel and proceeds to wash both the front and back panels in a cleaning solution, as well as the buttons. He also cleans the disassembled metal parts of the GameBoy with a cotton swab. He uses heat to remove the barcode sticker and replaces it with a new one. He cleans the lens as well.

Now he starts to reassemble the GameBoy with the help of an electronic screwdriver. He puts back the batteries as well as the cartridge. Now the GameBoy is in fully working condition; it starts the game without any issues and the audio works well. The repairman plays a bit of Pokémon as a demonstration. At the end of the video, we can see the fully restored GameBoy on a stand on the table.
